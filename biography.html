Sam Altman's Biography

Entrepreneur, investor, and technology visionary

Early Life and Education

  • Born in Chicago, Illinois on April 22, 1985, and grew up in St. Louis, Missouri.
  • His mother was a dermatologist and his father a real estate broker.
  • Received his first computer, an Apple Macintosh, at age eight, and started learning to code.
  • Attended John Burroughs School in Ladue, Missouri.
  • Studied computer science at Stanford University for two years before dropping out in 2005. He was involved in the AI laboratory there.

Career Beginnings

  • In 2005, co-founded Loopt, a mobile social networking service that raised over $30 million in venture capital.
  • Joined Y Combinator, a startup accelerator, in 2011.

Rise to Prominence

  • Became the president of Y Combinator in 2014, a role he held until 2019. Under his leadership, YC supported about 1,900 companies, including major names like Airbnb, Instacart, and Reddit.
  • Co-founded OpenAI in 2015 and became its CEO in 2019. He was briefly fired in 2023 but was quickly reinstated. OpenAI is known for major advancements in AI, including ChatGPT.
